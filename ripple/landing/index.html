ONE LIVING SUPPLY CHAIN, ONE STUDENT PER BUSINESS

🧑‍🌾🧑‍🌾🧑‍🌾 → 🚛🚛 → 🏪🏪🏪 🍽️🍽️🍽️ → 🧒👵🧑‍🦱… ×24

Students join from their phones and take a seat in a pixel town: 3 lemon farms, 2 wholesale depots, 3 grocery stores, 3 cafés — and 24 simulated townsfolk with private budgets shopping at the bottom. Empty seats play themselves. When one student raises a price, everyone else feels it — and the whole town animates it on the projector: trucks reroute, shelves empty, townsfolk walk away.

🦋 THE BUTTERFLY EFFECT IS REAL MATH, NOT NARRATION

Every round, the pure-function economy engine re-simulates the town without each student's move and diffs the two worlds. Demand curves emerge from 24 agents' willingness-to-pay — there is no D = a − bP formula anywhere.

🧑‍🌾 Maya's Farm raised crate price $3 → $6
→ felt by: Old Mill Farm +$36 · Big Crate Depot −$12 · 3 townsfolk priced out 😤

🧠 GRADED ON THINKING, NOT PROFIT — AND IT REMEMBERS YOU

🎯Goal — each student chases a secret role-specific objective (motivation, never the grade)
🧠Decision quality — an AI examiner (Nebius Llama-70B) grades each economic concept: supply shocks, tax incidence, hidden quality, cartels
🦋Ecosystem impact — counterfactual town-welfare delta caused by your moves
💾EverOS memory — skill models persist across sessions; next class, the orchestrator casts each student against their weakest skill
